## 2. Promoted To Authority Now

The following are promoted into BOSMAX immediately.

### A. Product truth over AI spectacle

AI may frame the sale, pace the beats, polish the scene, and scale variants.
AI must not become the proof itself.

Commercial proof must come from:
- real packaging truth
- real scale anchors
- observable use
- clear hand/product interaction
- label / object / task legibility

### B. UGC and PGC are different jobs

`UGC` is strongest for:
- relatability
- practical recommendation
- fast conversion
- friction removal

`PGC` is strongest for:
- clean premium object truth
- launch / prestige framing
- sensitive or compliance-heavy categories
- controlled visual sequencing

`HYBRID` is often strongest overall:
- creator-native open
- then product truth inserts, POV, hand detail, or hero frames

### C. Synthetic persona cannot act as fake proof

Synthetic humans may act as:
- host
- guide
- demonstrator
- scenario carrier

Synthetic humans should not act as:
- fake customer witness
- fake patient
- fake expert
- fake founder
- fake testimonial proof source

### D. Short-form product video must be function-led

Every beat must do one of these jobs:
- Hook
- Explain
- Prove
- Payoff
- CTA

If a shot does none of these, it is decorative waste.

### E. Multi-image authority must be hierarchical

Priority order:
1. product truth image
2. hero identity image
3. environment image
4. texture/style image
5. mood/B-roll support image

### F. B-roll is support, not filler

B-roll is valid only when it:
- proves a spoken claim
- supports scene context
- masks seam/extension transitions
- improves pacing without weakening truth

## 3. Strong Heuristics — Useful, But Not Hard Law Yet

These are valuable patterns, but BOSMAX should treat them as heuristics until
further verification or repeated field evidence.

### A. Duration shot ladders

Useful:
- 6s, 10s, 12s, 16s, 20s, 30s as distinct video architectures
- each duration needs different beat density and copy density

Not yet promoted as exact law:
- exact number of cuts per second
- exact number of words per minute from Gemini

### B. Camera-language taxonomy

Useful and promotable with caution:
- ECU
- CU
- MCU
- Medium
- Medium Wide
- Wide
- Insert
- Product Hero
- Hand Detail
- POV
- OTS
- Push-in / Pull-back / Pan / Tilt
- Match cut
- B-roll support shot

Not yet law:
- rigid shot quotas
- one-size-fits-all angle sequences

### C. Google Flow Ingredients advantage

Useful working assumption:
- Google Flow / Veo Ingredients is better than Grok for multi-image compositing
  and ingredient-driven scene building

Not yet law:
- exact deterministic timeline control from ingredients

### D. Grok multi-reference value

Useful working assumption:
- Grok multi-reference can help identity / object / scene consistency

Not yet law:
- exact shot-by-shot B-roll scheduling
- exact audio seam preservation from native generation alone

## 4. Rejected Or Not Promoted

The following are explicitly not promoted into BOSMAX as authority yet.

### A. Hard legal or platform claims without verified source chain

Reject as authority unless verified:
- exact Malaysia platform policy claims from Gemini without direct source
- universal C2PA enforcement assumptions
- exact mandatory marketplace disclaimer templates unless source-verified

### B. Hard numeric laws from Gemini

Do not promote yet:
- `130 words per minute`
- `1.5 to 2.5 cuts per second`
- `product must occupy 80% of canvas`
- `minimum 3 product reference images` as universal hard requirement

These may be useful guides, not deterministic law.

### C. Before-after reliance

For BOSMAX commercial video:
- before/after should never become default strategy
- treat as high-risk visual grammar unless category and platform are explicitly safe

## 5. BOSMAX Promotion Rules After Reconciliation

Research may only be promoted into live BOSMAX authority if it satisfies one of:

### Rule A — Verified

The point is supported by:
- official platform docs
- reliable official model documentation
- or consistent BOSMAX field evidence already observed

### Rule B — Strong cross-source heuristic

The point appears in both reports, does not conflict with known platform reality,
and is consistent with current BOSMAX field failures.

### Rule C — Explicitly non-authority

If neither A nor B is true:
- keep it as heuristic
- do not hard-code it into compliance or kill-switch rules
