# BOSMAX SHOT LADDER ARCHITECTURE v1

Authority file for multi-beat short-form product video design.

Purpose:
- stop static AI video
- stop random angle changes with no sales job
- define practical shot progression for UGC, PGC, and HYBRID product video

---

## 1. Core Law

Every beat must do one of these jobs:
- Hook
- Explain
- Prove
- Payoff
- CTA

Every angle change must be justified by one of:
- new information
- new emotional emphasis
- proof enhancement
- seam masking
- CTA reinforcement

Do not cut just to feel "cinematic".

---

## 2. Shot Function Map

| Shot | Main job | Best route |
|------|----------|------------|
| ECU | texture / micro-proof | `PGC` / `HYBRID` |
| CU | hook / expression / product emphasis | all |
| MCU | talk-to-camera explanation | `UGC` / `HYBRID` |
| Medium | gesture + product handling | `UGC` / `HYBRID` |
| Medium Wide | space + movement | all |
| Wide | establish place | `PGC` / `HYBRID` |
| Insert | proof | all |
| Product Hero | object truth / premium close | `PGC` / `HYBRID` |
| Hand Detail | use proof / scale proof | all |
| POV | first-person utility | `UGC` / `HYBRID` |
| Reaction | emotional validation | `UGC` |

---

## 3. Route Defaults

### UGC shot ladder tendency

- hook with person or POV
- move quickly to product-in-use
- keep one visible human anchor
- use product detail only when it serves proof

### PGC shot ladder tendency

- open with product or controlled context
- use inserts and hero frames more deliberately
- reduce synthetic-human dependence

### HYBRID shot ladder tendency

- hook with creator-native or POV
- prove with object-led inserts
- close with either creator CTA or object CTA

## 4. Duration Ladders

## 6s

### UGC
- Beat 1: Hook — CU/MCU/POV
- Beat 2: Proof — hand detail or quick insert
- Beat 3: CTA — creator point or product hold

### PGC
- Beat 1: Product hero or macro hook
- Beat 2: one proof insert
- Beat 3: clean CTA close

### HYBRID
- Beat 1: creator or POV hook
- Beat 2: product proof insert
- Beat 3: object-led CTA

---

## 10s

### UGC
- Beat 1: Hook
- Beat 2: Pain / problem context
- Beat 3: Product enters
- Beat 4: Relief / proof
- Beat 5: CTA

Recommended shot ladder:
- MCU -> Medium or POV -> Hand Detail -> CU Insert -> MCU CTA

### PGC
- Beat 1: clean context or hero object
- Beat 2: feature emphasis
- Beat 3: use proof
- Beat 4: tactile or design close-up
- Beat 5: CTA

### HYBRID
- Beat 1: creator/native hook
- Beat 2: object reveal
- Beat 3: proof insert
- Beat 4: payoff
- Beat 5: CTA

---

## 12s

### Default ladder
- Beat 1: Hook
- Beat 2: Pain
- Beat 3: Product reveal
- Beat 4: Proof
- Beat 5: CTA preload
- Beat 6: CTA close

Good use:
- `6s + 6s` Grok
- Block 1 = Hook + Pain + bridge-out
- Block 2 = bridge-in + Proof + CTA

---

## 16s

### Default ladder
- Beat 1: Hook
- Beat 2: Pain
- Beat 3: Friction
- Beat 4: Product reveal
- Beat 5: Proof
- Beat 6: Relief / payoff
- Beat 7: CTA

Good Grok structure:
- `10s + 6s`
- Block 1: Hook + Pain + Friction + bridge-out
- Block 2: bridge-in + Proof/Relief + CTA

Suggested HYBRID shot ladder:
- MCU hook -> POV frustration -> product-in-hand -> insert -> hand detail -> reaction or calm payoff -> CTA

---

## 20s

### Default ladder
- Beat 1: Hook
- Beat 2: Pain
- Beat 3: Friction
- Beat 4: Product reveal
- Beat 5: Mechanism or use
- Beat 6: Proof
- Beat 7: Benefit clarification
- Beat 8: CTA

Good Grok structure:
- `10s + 10s`
- Block 1: Hook + Pain + Friction + bridge-out
- Block 2: bridge-in + Relief + proof/detail + CTA

This is the strongest default duration for:
- direct-response HYBRID
- practical household
- object-truth-led UGC/PGC mixes

---

## 30s

### Default ladder
- Beat 1: Hook
- Beat 2: Setup
- Beat 3: Pain
- Beat 4: Friction
- Beat 5: Product reveal
- Beat 6: Mechanism
- Beat 7: Proof
- Beat 8: Secondary proof or detail
- Beat 9: Payoff
- Beat 10: CTA

This duration is only justified when:
- product requires explanation
- proof needs more than one visual layer
- the route is `PGC` or `HYBRID`

Do not use 30s just because the engine can.

---

## 5. Angle Change Rules

Angle changes are recommended when:
- the next beat has a different sales job
- the viewer needs closer proof
- the seam between extension blocks needs masking
- the product needs scale or grip clarification

Angle changes are not recommended when:
- the current frame is already doing the job
- the object is tiny and drift risk is high
- the new angle adds no new information

---

## 6. Static Risk Rule

The video risks feeling static if:
- the same frame class holds too long
- dialogue continues while no new proof appears
- the product stays in one position without a beat reason

Fix with:
- insert
- hand detail
- POV
- reaction
- controlled push-in
- micro product repositioning tied to a spoken idea

---

## 7. Seam Masking Rule

For multi-block generation:
- do not begin Block 2 with a new angle unless storyboard justifies it
- if the seam is weak, use:
  - micro-continuation
  - insert bridge
  - hand-detail bridge
  - POV bridge

The first job of Block 2 is continuity.
The second job is new information.

---

## 8. Operator Output Requirement

Every storyboard should declare:
- route = `UGC | PGC | HYBRID`
- beat count
- shot ladder
- which beats are proof beats
- where CTA lands
